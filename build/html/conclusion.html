Conclusion¶

While Robert had his own unique challenges and did not complete the full track, we are proud of what we were able to accomplish over the course of the quarter. The project provided valuable hands-on experience in integrating mechanical design, electrical systems, and embedded software into a fully functioning robot.

Throughout the process, we developed a much stronger understanding of MicroPython programming, real-time control, and task-based system design. We also gained practical experience with debugging complex systems, where issues could arise from hardware, software, or the interaction between the two.

One of the most important lessons from this project was the importance of iterative testing and design decisions. Small choices, such as sensor placement or hardware configuration, had a significant impact on overall performance. This reinforced the need to think carefully about system-level design rather than treating each component independently.

Despite not completing the full course, Robert demonstrated consistent performance in several key areas, including reliable straight-line tracking and successful execution of the garage sequence. These successes highlight the strength of our control strategy and overall system design.

Overall, this project was both challenging and rewarding. We gained confidence in our ability to design, build, and troubleshoot a complex mechatronic system, and we had a lot of fun working with the Romi platform throughout the process.
