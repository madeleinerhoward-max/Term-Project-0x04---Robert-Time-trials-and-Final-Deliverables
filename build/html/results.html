Course Performance¶

As mentioned previously, Robert was able to reach as far as checkpoint 3 on the track. However, he did not complete the full course in any trial, resulting in a DNF (Did Not Finish) for all runs. Despite this, the robot demonstrated consistent and repeatable performance in the earlier sections of the course.

From checkpoint 0 to checkpoint 1, which consists of straight-line tracking, Robert reliably completes this section in approximately 11–12 seconds. This portion of the course highlights the effectiveness of the line-following algorithm under stable conditions.

When the garage sequence is successfully executed, Robert is able to complete it in approximately 24–30 seconds. This variation in time is primarily due to differences in alignment and sensor triggering, but overall the sequence remains consistent and repeatable.

In the zig-zag portion of the track, Robert operates at a significantly reduced speed in order to maintain accurate line tracking. While this results in slower performance, it improves reliability in navigating tighter curves. Due to the robot’s size and geometry, it is able to successfully interact with and fit all three ping pong balls during each run, even though it does not complete the entire course.

---

Performance Summary¶

  • Checkpoint 0 → 1: ~11–12 seconds (consistent)

  • Garage sequence: ~24–30 seconds (when successfully triggered)

  • Zig-zag section: Slow but stable tracking

  • Final result: DNF in all trials (max checkpoint reached: 3)
